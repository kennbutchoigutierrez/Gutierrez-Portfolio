A living moodboard and reference doc for the Gutierrez portfolio. Use this to keep design and content decisions consistent as the site comes together.

## Goal

A portfolio that feels intentional and personal — not a template. It should load fast, read clearly, and leave a visitor with a strong sense of who Gutierrez is and what they do.

## Direction / Vibe

Pick one primary direction and commit to it. Options to consider:

- Editorial minimal — Generous whitespace, one strong typeface, restrained color. Content does the talking. (Think a well-set magazine.)
- Bold & expressive — Large display type, a confident accent color, motion on scroll. Memorable, opinionated.
- Warm & human — Softer palette, rounded shapes, photography of real work/life. Approachable.
- Technical / systems — Monospace accents, grid-forward layout, subtle terminal aesthetic. Good for a developer.

## Reference Sites

Sites worth studying for layout, typography, and interaction (add links as you find them):

- Personal portfolios with a single memorable idea rather than a feature list
- Case-study pages that tell a story: problem → approach → outcome
- Award galleries (Awwwards, Godly, Land-book) for current visual trends

## Typography

- Display / headings: TBD — a typeface with personality for the hero and section titles
- Body: TBD — highly legible at small sizes, good at 16–18px
- Aim for strong contrast between heading and body sizes; establish a clear type scale.

## Color

- Choose a neutral base (near-black + off-white) and one accent.
- Ensure text meets WCAG AA contrast in both light and dark.
- Define the palette once as tokens/variables and reuse everywhere.

## Layout & Structure

Sections most portfolios need:

1. Hero — Name, one-line what-you-do, a clear next action.
2. About — Short bio, a photo, what you care about.
3. Work / Projects — 3–6 strong pieces, each with a thumbnail and a one-line result.
4. Skills / Tools — Brief, scannable.

Keep it to what's needed. One great project beats five mediocre ones.

## Interaction & Motion

- Motion should support hierarchy, not decorate. Subtle entrance fades, smooth scroll, hover feedback.
- Respect prefers-reduced-motion.
- Everything usable by keyboard; visible focus states.

## Content Principles

- Write in first person, plainly. Cut filler.
- Lead every project with the outcome, then the how.
- Show real work over stock imagery wherever possible.
